Factors affecting your health insurance premium
Not many are aware of different factors that determine the premium amount for an insurance policy.
• Medical history
Insurance companies look at you and your family’s medical history. If there are certain medical ailments in the family, the premium amount will be higher.
• Pre-existing conditions
You will have to provide your health records in order to ensure that you do not suffer from any pre-existing conditions. There are certain companies that allow such conditions in their policies while there are many others who do not agree to cover it, hence, you will have to cover for the costs and this could increase the premium amount.
• Gender
Insurance companies have different rates for men and women. It is believed that women are more likely to visit doctors and are subject to chronic illnesses; hence the premium will be higher for women.
• Injurious substances
If you have a habit of chewing tobacco or smoking, the premium amount will be higher as compared to those who have no such habits of injurious substances. This is because you are prone to getting a chronic illness like cancer.
• Previous insurance history
If you are not previously covered, you will have to pay a higher amount of premium. Insurers believe that those individuals who have not been covered by a policy in the past would make frequent trips to the doctors in order to reap the benefits of the policy. Hence, the premium will be higher in order to cover all the expenses.
